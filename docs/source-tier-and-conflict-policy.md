# Source Tier and Conflict Policy

## Source Tiers

| Tier | Source Type | Allowed Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| T1 | User-confirmed data, signed-off engineering documents, official product datasheets | May support product facts, specifications, and public wording after review. |
| T2 | KingPo-owned websites and catalogs | May support product facts, but technical parameters and standards claims still need field-level evidence. |
| T3 | Official standards bodies and regulators such as IEC, ISO, EN/CEN/CENELEC/ETSI, UL, UN ECE, SASO, BIS, ASTM, ANSI/AAMI, IEEE | May support public standard titles, scope, family, and high-level relevance. Do not copy copyrighted standard text or invent clauses. |
| T4 | Public industry education, buyer guides, non-competitor references | May support background explanation and buyer questions. |
| T5 | Competitor sites, marketplaces, third-party scraped content | Research only. Never use as KingPo product fact, image, certification, customer proof, or parameter source. |

## Conflict Resolution

When sources disagree:

1. Prefer the newest user-confirmed or engineering-reviewed source.
2. Mark the affected field as `conflict_detected`.
3. Preserve all source IDs in the record.
4. Do not publish the disputed claim until a reviewer resolves it.
5. Record the decision and reviewer in the claim or specification record.

## Old Site Rule

KingPo-owned legacy pages are useful, but not automatically verified. Old pages may contain outdated model names, images, standards, parameters, or marketing claims. Treat them as source evidence, not final truth.
