# Standard Version and Claim Policy

## Standards Source Rule

When existing data is incomplete, use official or authoritative sources for public standard titles, organizations, scope summaries, and high-level relevance:

- IEC
- ISO
- EN / CEN / CENELEC / ETSI
- UL
- UN ECE
- SASO
- BIS
- ASTM
- ANSI/AAMI
- IEEE
- other official standards organizations where relevant

Do not copy copyrighted standard text, tables, acceptance criteria, or test procedures.

## Version and Clause Rule

Do not invent:

- standard editions;
- clause numbers;
- test conditions;
- acceptance criteria;
- required fixtures;
- KingPo compliance conclusions.

If the exact edition or clause is unknown, mark the field as `needs_confirmation` and avoid publishing clause-specific claims.

## Product-Standard Relationship

A standard page may explain the standard family and test direction. It does not prove any product meets the standard.

A public product-standard claim requires a `standard_mapping` record with:

- product ID;
- standard ID and edition;
- clause or test item if used publicly;
- relationship type;
- coverage scope;
- required configuration;
- source IDs;
- reviewer;
- review status;
- approved public wording.
