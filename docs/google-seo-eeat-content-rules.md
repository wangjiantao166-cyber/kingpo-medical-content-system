# Google SEO, E-E-A-T, and Customer-First Content Rules

## Purpose

This document defines the content quality rules for KingPo product, category, application, standard, and technical article pages.

The goal is to create helpful, reliable, people-first content that can support Google organic search, AI search visibility, and overseas B2B buyer trust without exaggerating product capability or copying competitors.

## Core Google SEO Principles

Content must be built for users first and search engines second.

Google Search documentation emphasizes that SEO should help search engines understand content and help users find useful pages. SEO must support people-first content rather than replace it.

For KingPo pages, this means:

- Write for real engineers, buyers, lab managers, QA/regulatory staff, and test equipment decision-makers.
- Make the page answer the buyer's practical questions quickly.
- Use clear page titles, headings, product names, standard references, and internal links.
- Avoid thin, generic, keyword-stuffed, or mass-produced text.
- Avoid creating many near-identical pages with only keywords or model names changed.

## E-E-A-T Rules

E-E-A-T means Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. For this project, E-E-A-T must be demonstrated through source quality, technical accuracy, cautious claims, and useful buyer guidance.

### Experience

Show practical understanding of how labs, manufacturers, certification bodies, and R&D teams use the equipment.

Good signals:

- Test object and sample type are explained.
- Test workflow or test purpose is clear.
- Selection/configuration questions match real buyer needs.
- Inquiry forms ask for standard, sample, test item, and parameter information.

Avoid abstract marketing claims with no testing context.

### Expertise

Show technical understanding without overclaiming.

Good signals:

- Standards are classified carefully.
- Test principle is explained at the right depth.
- Parameters are only included when sourced.
- Unknown parameters are marked as needing confirmation or omitted.

Avoid invented technical parameters, unsupported standard versions, unsupported clause numbers, and unsupported phrases such as `fully complies with`, `guaranteed to pass`, or `certified for`.

### Authoritativeness

Build authority through original KingPo-specific content and clear source ownership.

Good signals:

- Product facts come from KingPo-owned or user-confirmed sources.
- Competitor information is used only for market learning, not as KingPo fact.
- Product pages link to related standards, applications, technical articles, and approved PDFs.

Avoid copying competitor pages, tables, images, FAQs, or model structures.

### Trustworthiness

Trust is the highest priority for medical test equipment content.

Good signals:

- Product status is clear.
- R&D/reference directions are labeled honestly.
- Source notes and human confirmation status are recorded.
- Contact, inquiry, privacy, and company information are accessible.
- High-risk claims are avoided unless verified.

Avoid fake customer cases, fake certifications, fake inventory, price, lead time, registration claims, medical claims, or clinical outcome claims.

## Customer-First B2B Product Page Rules

Every product page should help a buyer answer:

1. What is this equipment?
2. What test need does it solve?
3. Which standard or test method direction is it related to?
4. What sample or product is tested?
5. What test items or parameters matter?
6. Is this an existing product, custom development direction, or R&D reference?
7. What information should I provide to request a quote or solution?

## Flexible Page Structure Rule

Do not force every product into one identical template.

The core information must be complete, but the module order below Product Overview should adapt to the product type, buyer intent, and available evidence.

Possible modules include:

- Key Features
- Test Principle
- Standard Compliance / Standard Direction
- Applications
- Test Objects
- Configuration Notes
- Selection Guide
- Technical Specifications
- Software and Control
- Fixtures and Accessories
- PDF Downloads
- Related Products
- FAQ
- Inquiry Preparation Checklist

Do not add a module only to fill space. Do not repeat the same idea across multiple sections.

## Standard and Compliance Wording Rules

Use cautious standard wording.

Preferred wording:

- Related to the test requirements of ...
- Designed for evaluating ...
- Can be configured for test scenarios involving ...
- Standard applicability should be confirmed based on sample type, standard version, and test conditions.
- The final test method and acceptance criteria should be confirmed by the user, laboratory, or certification body.

Avoid unless verified:

- Fully complies with ...
- Certified to ...
- Guaranteed to pass ...
- Meets all requirements of ...
- Approved for registration testing ...

## AI-Generated Content Rules

AI may help draft content, but AI output must be reviewed as draft material.

AI-generated product pages must:

- Preserve technical facts from source material.
- Separate confirmed facts from background explanation.
- Mark uncertain fields as needing confirmation.
- Avoid invented parameters, standards, clauses, certifications, cases, prices, stock, delivery time, or test results.
- Avoid copying competitors.
- Use natural professional English for overseas B2B buyers.
- Keep content concise enough for buyers to scan.

## S-CTs: Search Content Trust Signals

For this repository, S-CTs means Search Content Trust Signals. These are page-level signals that improve trust for users and search engines.

Every important page should include as many relevant S-CTs as possible:

- Clear product status
- Source ownership classification
- Human verification status
- Related standards with relationship type
- Test object and test item clarity
- Technical parameters with evidence or pending status
- Approved product images or diagrams
- PDF/catalog version date if available
- FAQ based on real buyer questions
- Internal links to related products, applications, standards, and articles
- Inquiry CTA that asks for technical requirements
- Last review date for important technical pages

## Google Structured Data Rules

Use structured data only when the visible page content supports it.

Recommended schema considerations:

- Product structured data for confirmed product pages.
- Organization structured data for company information.
- Breadcrumb structured data for navigation.
- FAQ structured data only when FAQ content is visible and valid for current Google guidance.

Do not add fake price, availability, review, rating, shipping, or return data. For B2B custom equipment, many merchant fields may be inappropriate unless the business truly provides those details.

Structured data must be validated before production release.

## Page Experience and Mobile-First Rules

Google uses mobile-first indexing, so the mobile version must contain equivalent important content.

Check:

- Product overview, specs, standards, CTAs, and FAQs are visible on mobile.
- Tables are readable or transformed into mobile-friendly sections.
- Images have useful alt text.
- Pages load quickly.
- Navigation and internal links are usable on mobile.
- No important content is hidden only on mobile.

## Core Update Resilience

Google core updates are broad ranking system changes. The safest long-term strategy is not chasing tricks, but improving helpfulness, reliability, originality, and user satisfaction.

For KingPo, maintain:

- Original product explanations.
- Real source records.
- Updated standards relationships.
- Useful application pages.
- Clean internal links.
- No unsupported claims.
- No duplicated mass content.

## Content QA Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- The page answers real customer questions.
- The page has a clear product/category/application/standard intent.
- Technical facts have sources.
- Unconfirmed fields are marked or omitted.
- Standards wording is cautious.
- Product status is correct.
- The content is not copied from competitors.
- The page has useful internal links.
- The CTA matches buyer needs.
- SEO title and meta description are unique.
- Mobile layout preserves important content.
- Structured data, if used, matches visible page content.
